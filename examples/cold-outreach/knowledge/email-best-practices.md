# Cold Email Best Practices

## Subject Line Formulas (40%+ Open Rates)

Winning subject line patterns based on analysis of 10M+ cold emails:

- **[Name/Company] + [specific reference]**: "Saw [Company]'s new launch" (42% avg open rate)
- **Quick question format**: "Quick question about [their initiative]" (45% avg open rate)
- **Mutual connection**: "[Name] suggested I reach out" (51% avg open rate)
- **Curiosity gap**: "Idea for [Company]'s [specific area]" (39% avg open rate)
- **One word / ultra-short**: "Thoughts?" or "[First name]?" (44% avg open rate, use sparingly)

**Rules:** Keep subject lines under 7 words. Never use ALL CAPS, exclamation marks, or spam trigger words (free, guaranteed, act now). Avoid "Re:" or "Fwd:" tricks — they erode trust.

## Optimal Email Length

- **Sweet spot: 50-125 words.** Emails in this range see **2x the response rate** of emails over 200 words.
- First sentence: max 10 words (hooks attention in preview pane)
- One core idea per email — don't try to say everything
- White space matters: short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)
- If you can't cut it to 125 words, you're pitching too hard

## Best Send Times

Based on aggregated data across B2B cold email platforms:

| Day | Open Rate | Reply Rate |
|-----|-----------|------------|
| **Tuesday** | 21.5% | 4.1% |
| **Wednesday** | 21.2% | 3.9% |
| **Thursday** | 20.8% | 3.8% |
| Monday | 19.1% | 3.2% |
| Friday | 18.4% | 2.9% |

- **Optimal send window:** 8:00-10:00 AM in the **recipient's timezone**
- **Secondary window:** 1:00-2:00 PM (post-lunch inbox check)
- **Avoid:** Weekends, holidays, Friday afternoons, Monday mornings before 8 AM

## Follow-Up Cadence

Most replies come from follow-ups, not the initial email. Recommended sequence:

1. **Day 0**: Initial email
2. **Day 3**: First follow-up — add new value or angle, don't just "bump"
3. **Day 7**: Second follow-up — share a relevant resource or case study
4. **Day 14**: Final follow-up — short breakup email ("Should I close the loop?")

- **80% of positive replies** come on follow-ups 1-3, not the initial send
- Each follow-up should add new information, not repeat the original
- The breakup email ("Is this not a priority right now? Totally fine — just don't want to clutter your inbox") often gets the highest reply rate (6-8%)

## Personalization Techniques

Tiers of personalization (in order of effectiveness):

1. **Hyper-personalized** (best): Reference a specific LinkedIn post, podcast appearance, product feature, or recent company event. Response rate: 15-25%
2. **Role-personalized**: Reference challenges specific to their role/title. "As a VP Eng, you're probably dealing with..." Response rate: 8-14%
3. **Company-personalized**: Reference their company's industry, size, or stage. Response rate: 5-10%
4. **Segment-personalized** (minimum viable): Reference their industry vertical. Response rate: 3-6%
5. **Generic** (avoid): No personalization. Response rate: 1-2%

## CTA Formulas

Effective calls-to-action for cold outreach:

- **Low friction (best):** "Worth a 10-minute call this week?" or "Reply 'sure' and I'll send details"
- **Interest-based:** "Want me to send over the case study?" or "Curious to see how it works?"
- **Binary choice:** "Would Tuesday or Thursday work better?"
- **Avoid:** Long scheduling links in first email, "Let me know your thoughts" (too vague), multiple CTAs
