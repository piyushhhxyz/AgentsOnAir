# VC Landscape — Top Firms & Investment Theses (2024)

## Tier 1 — Multi-Stage
- **a16z (Andreessen Horowitz)**: AI infrastructure, crypto, bio, gaming. Recent: AI agents, foundation models. Partners: Marc Andreessen, Ben Horowitz, Martin Casado (infra), Vijay Pande (bio)
- **Sequoia Capital**: Consumer, enterprise SaaS, fintech, AI. "We help the daring build legendary companies." Partners: Roelof Botha, Alfred Lin, Jess Lee
- **Benchmark**: Early-stage only, equal partnership. Consumer, marketplaces, SaaS. Partners: Bill Gurley, Sarah Tavel, Peter Fenton
- **Founders Fund**: Contrarian bets, deep tech, AI, defense. "We wanted flying cars, instead we got 140 characters." Partners: Peter Thiel, Keith Rabois, Delian Asparouhov

## Tier 1 — Early Stage / Seed
- **Y Combinator**: Batch model, $500K standard deal. Thesis: bet on founders, not ideas. Recent focus: AI, dev tools, B2B SaaS
- **First Round Capital**: Seed stage, strong community. Thesis: company building from Day 1. Known for: Uber, Square, Notion
- **Initialized Capital (Garry Tan)**: Pre-seed/seed, technical founders. Now Garry runs YC.

## AI-Focused Funds
- **AI Grant**: Small grants for AI research/projects
- **Conviction Partners**: AI-native companies only. Sarah Guo (ex-Greylock)
- **Radical Ventures**: AI moonshots, deep tech. Jordan Jacobs, Tad Soper

## What VCs Look For in Cold Emails
1. **Traction first**: Revenue, users, growth rate — not the idea
2. **Why you**: Founder-market fit, unique insight, unfair advantage
3. **Why now**: Market timing, regulatory shift, tech inflection point
4. **Why this VC**: Specific thesis alignment, portfolio synergy
5. **Social proof**: Notable customers, advisors, angels already in

## Red Flags That Kill Cold Emails
- No metrics / "pre-revenue, pre-product"
- Generic blast to 200 VCs (they can tell)
- Asking for NDA before sharing the idea
- "We have no competition"
- Attaching a 30-slide deck without being asked
