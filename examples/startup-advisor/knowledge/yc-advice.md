# YC Startup Advice — Key Principles

## Paul Graham's Essentials
- Make something people want. That's the core of it.
- Talk to users. Not 100 users — talk to 5 deeply.
- Do things that don't scale. Manual work first, automate later.
- Launch fast. If you're not embarrassed by v1, you launched too late.
- Growth rate is everything. 7% week-over-week is good. 10% is great.

## Common Mistakes YC Sees
1. **Building before talking to users** — 90% of failed startups built something nobody wanted
2. **Too many features** — Ship one thing that works, not ten things that kinda work
3. **Premature scaling** — Don't hire, don't spend on ads, until you have product-market fit
4. **Cofounders who don't code** — At least one founder must be technical
5. **Solving your own problem vs. a real market** — Your problem might not be anyone else's

## What Makes Ideas Fundable
- **Large market** ($1B+ TAM)
- **Founder-market fit** — Why are YOU the right person to build this?
- **Clear distribution** — How do the first 1,000 users find you?
- **Defensibility** — Network effects, data moats, switching costs
- **Timing** — Why now? What changed that makes this possible today?

## Metrics That Matter (Pre-Seed)
- Weekly active users (WAU)
- Retention (Week 1, Week 4, Week 8)
- NPS > 50
- Organic growth / word-of-mouth ratio
- Revenue (even $1 of revenue > $0)
