# Research Methodology Framework

## Source Hierarchy
1. **Primary Sources** (Credibility: High)
   - Official documentation, whitepapers, peer-reviewed papers
   - Direct data from APIs, databases, official statistics
   - First-party announcements and press releases

2. **Secondary Sources** (Credibility: Medium)
   - Industry reports (Gartner, McKinsey, a16z)
   - Reputable tech publications (TechCrunch, The Verge, Ars Technica)
   - Expert blog posts from recognized authorities

3. **Tertiary Sources** (Credibility: Low)
   - Social media posts, Reddit threads, HN comments
   - Unverified blog posts, opinion pieces
   - Aggregator sites without original reporting

## Cross-Reference Protocol
- Every key finding must be supported by 2+ independent sources
- Contradictions between sources must be explicitly flagged
- Recency bias check: prefer sources from last 6 months
- Author credibility: check track record and affiliations

## Confidence Scoring
- **High (0.8-1.0)**: Multiple primary sources agree, recent data
- **Medium (0.5-0.7)**: Mixed sources, some contradictions, older data
- **Low (0.0-0.4)**: Single source, unverified, speculative

## Output Quality Checklist
- [ ] Executive summary is self-contained (readable without rest of brief)
- [ ] Every finding has a confidence score
- [ ] Sources are cited with credibility ratings
- [ ] Contradictions are explicitly called out
- [ ] Knowledge gaps are identified
- [ ] Next steps are actionable and specific
